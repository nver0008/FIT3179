2021-22 Football Season Transfer Review

Reviewing last year's transfer windows!

Transfers by Players

If you are unfamiliarly with a transfer fee, when a player is still contracted with a club for an upcoming season, a potential buyer will make a monetary offer to the club the player is contracted to for their contract to be terminated. From there, they are free to sign with the new club and discuss wages and bonuses. A player's value is dependent on a number of factors, including (but not limited to) age, form, injury history and career potential.

In this section, we will review individual player movements at the end of the 2020-21 season. Note the large number of players being signed by a premier league club and are English. This is due to what is colloquially known as the "English Tax". This is because in the Premier League, every club is required to have a certain number of English players, and as such talented english players are a commodity and therefore clubs will pay higher fees to obtain their signatures.

Club Expenditure

In this section, we explore the total expenditure of all the top clubs across the top 5 leagues; that being the Premier League (England + Wales), Bundesliga (Germany), La Liga (Spain), Serie A (Italy) and Ligue 1 (France). Typically, clubs with more buying power are those that have stronger backing by owners and have global awareness.

Manchester United and Arsenal are both hugely successful english clubs, and Manchester City are backed heavily financially in more recent times and have enjoyed massive domestic success.

AS Roma spent big this window with some young talents, as did RB Leipzig as they challenge for the Bundesliga title, still looking for thier maiden trophy.

It is no surpirse to see Paris Saint-Germain amongst the top spenders, but Stade Rennais and Aston Villa are both clubs that spent big this summer and don't typically spend that much on player deals. That being said, Aston Villa were the ones that sold record-breaking Jack Grealish, and therefore had some cash to spend to make up for the loss of their captain.

Nations competing in 2021-22

For the top 5 leagues, typically the players are sourced from Europe, especially those from their home nation. Outside of Europe, clubs tend to look to the Americas, in particular Argentina and Brazil, as well as in more recent times, the USA, in search of talent. Western Africa is growing in terms of number of players in the elite divisions and Asia and Oceania is lagging behind massively.
